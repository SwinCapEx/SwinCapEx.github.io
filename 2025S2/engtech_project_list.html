ENG40008 Engineering Project A/B

This is a final year capstone project-based unit in which students work individually, or in teams if the project is a large industry project, to apply their skills to generate an engineering technology solution to an industry challenge. The project is part 2 of a year-long project.

    Students: Mohammad Lihanur Ifty, Syed Riduan Rashid

    Supervisor: Afshin Hosseini

    Client: Jimmy ho, Universal Biosensors

    This project designs an automated Pressure Decay Testing (PDT) system to improve leak detection in pharmaceutical and biosensing vials.   • UG_ENG_24 Prediction of Voltage Fluctuation in Power Distribution Networks with High Penetration of Renewable Energy (EN204-5)

    Students: Sathira Bathan Arachchige, Kumod Samarasinghe

    Supervisor: Jingxin Zhang

    Client: Jingxin Zhang, Swinburne University of Technology

    "Accurate short-term solar power forecasting is crucial for efficient grid management and renewable energy integration. Photovoltaic generation is inherently intermittent and influenced by weather conditions, posing challenges for reliable prediction. In this study, we systematically evaluate multiple temporal encoding schemes (traditional, sinusoidal, and cyclic) and model architectures, including CNNs, transformer-based models, hybrid CNN-LSTM approaches, and LSTM networks. Experiments on the UniSolar dataset demonstrate that cyclic temporal encoding combined with an LSTM-based model achieves superior predictive performance, capturing both short- and long-term temporal dependencies while remaining computationally efficient. Time-of-day analysis highlights robust performance across different periods, particularly during rapid changes in solar generation. Our results provide practical insights into model selection and temporal feature engineering for solar power forecasting."
