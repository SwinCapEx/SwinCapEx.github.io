COS40006 Computing Technology Project B (Games)

In this unit, teams work with students from GAM30002/3 Games & Interactivity Project 1/2 to develop their own games. This teams have already had the opportunity to present their demos at PAX 2025.

  • UG_GAM_01 Style Slasher (EN203)

    Students: Hugh Craig, Stella Davies, Marnus Jordaan, Ian Scheepers, Jaime Shirazi

    Supervisor: Q Tien Pham

    Style Slasher is a fashion roguelike, where you mix and match your clothes to gain powerful abilities and synergies, while finding your perfectly unique style. Explore the dungeon within your dreams to find new clothing pieces by defeating the fashion demons within! Each clothing item has a Style which dictates it's ability, and an Influence which gives that styles ability a modifier. Players can mix a variety of styles to have a powerful range of abilities at their disposal, or co-ordinate their outfit to have one ultra strong ability. Sweet dreams, and may you live up to your fashion potential!

  • UG_GAM_02 Feed the Dungeon (EN203)

    Students: Ned Madigan, Orson Routt, Duy Minh Tran, Hai Phuong Linh Vu

    Supervisor: Q Tien Pham

    Feed the Dungeon is a Dungeon-Crawler, and Hack-and-Slash game, starring the Raccoon Cook, Chef, whose ferocious appetite leads them deep within the Dungeon. Feed the Dungeon aims to give players a semi-cosy experience as they venture through a dungeon, slaying monsters and bosses and cooking them into meals to gain buffs. Combat is the core of this game but it revolves around the buffs you pick from your meals. The atmosphere is a fantastical and whimsy adventure set inside a rundown dungeon that has been taken over by all manner of monsters. Venture forth brave Chef, eat, work, slay, repeat!

  • UG_GAM_03 Gizmo (ATC Foyer)

  • Shark Tank recipient

    UG_GAM_05 Neo-Phalem (EN203)

    Students: Sam Briggs, Thomas Crow, Avery Flannery, Hamish Luke, Myn Ma, Raza Syed, Kevin Trevascus

    Supervisor: Q Tien Pham

    A Roleplaying game taking place in hell where you play as a fallen angel, in the game there is both turnbased and realtime combat, the player is able to use a range of different abilities to fight both demons and angels. The game's demo is targetted for a Windows and Linux releases with support for players to use a keyboard and mouse or a controller. In the demo, you will rescue your character's handler from a boss enemy while fighting demons and angelic constructs all while learning the mechanics of the game.
