  • UG_COSEAT_14 PallaShare -- A Community-Based Goods Sharing Platform (2)

    Students: Sabri Bayanta, Callan Bertram, Ashley Burston, Siradanai Inchansuk, Ryan Lovegrove, Dee Sethi

    Supervisor: Humphrey Obie

    Client: Md Muktar Hossain, Department of Computing Technologies, Swinburne

    Pallashare is a web application that aims to have users be able to buy and sell products at a lower than retail cost, with a specialty on bulk products. In the current cost of living crisis, people tend to buy items in bulk to save money. However, sometimes people don’t need the extra items, which can lead to a wastage of items. Pallashare aims to solve this by allowing users to both buy and sell individual items at the wholesale rates. This both saves money for buyers and reduces unnecessary hoarding of surplus items for sellers.

  • UG_COSEAT_29 Designing Australia's Central Bank Digital Currency (eAUD CBDC) (15)

    Students: Al Hamid Arath, Farzana Moietry, Jayakodige Lasith Charuka Perera, Ashikur Rahman, Luvish Rajnath, Isar Ujoodah

    Supervisor: Humphrey Obie

    Client: Md Sameen Chisti, Norwegian Institute of Science & Technology

    The eAUD CBDC project is a blockchain-based central bank digital currency system for Australia, built on Hyperledger Fabric. The system includes four organizations: RBA (central bank), BankA and BankB (commercial banks), and AUSTRAC (regulator). It provides role-based dashboards for five user types with real-time eAUD transfers, KYC document upload before wallet creation, AML transaction monitoring with 7 suspicious detection rules, risk scoring, and flagged accounts watchlist. The system integrates Keycloak for enterprise-grade SSO authentication.

    Supervisor: Md Muktar Hossain

    Client: Alex Jaimes Castillo, Swinburne University of Technology

    The LIDA composting machine is an innovative solution to a generational waste crisis. It employs fans, stirrers, and gas and temperature sensors to facilitate the fine-tuning of the composting process, broken into three stages for enhanced efficiency. It can also be remotely controlled by a mobile app; for this project, we have worked on that app and created a web-based version with identical UI and functionality. We have overseen the app's transformation into a functional, attractive, accessible, secure, multi-lingual and multi-platform program that is ready for the modern world. This project involved a Flutter/Dart codebase with web (AWS) connections, and targets Android, iOS, and the web.
